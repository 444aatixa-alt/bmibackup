üî¨ Key Example Sensors & Their Testing Equipment
01

Heart Sensor (ECG)

‚ö°
Testing Equipment: Electrical Simulators ‚Äî because real human heart rates naturally fluctuate, a simulator sends a perfectly controlled, mathematically precise electrical signal that mimics a human heart. This gives engineers a reliable, repeatable input to test against.
‚öôÔ∏è 1.1 ‚Äî Analysers & Simulators

The primary classification of an electrical simulator. It acts as a controlled stand-in for the patient ‚Äî the benchmark the device is measured against.

KPIs Targeted

Precision & Response Time

üìå Use Case: Set the simulator to exactly 60 BPM ‚Äî the device must display 60.0 BPM every single time without deviation.
üî¨ 1.2 ‚Äî Functional Testing

Simulators verify the entire system "works" end-to-end ‚Äî from the physical electrode leads all the way through to the software display.

KPIs Targeted

Sensitivity & Accuracy

üìå Use Case: Trigger a simulated arrhythmia (irregular heartbeat) ‚Äî verify the software detects the anomaly and fires an alarm.
‚ö° 1.3 ‚Äî Electrical Safety Testing

Simulators are paired with safety analyzers to perform electrical safety checks ‚Äî ensuring no harmful electrical current can reach the patient.

KPIs Targeted

Repeatability

üìå Use Case: Check for "Leakage Current" ‚Äî confirms that no unsafe electrical flow can pass through the device to the patient.
02

SpO‚ÇÇ ‚Äî Pulse Oximetry

üí°
How it works: Uses two wavelengths of light (red & infrared) shone through the fingertip to measure oxygen saturation in the blood. Testing Equipment: Pulse Oximetry Testers ‚Äî these mimic the human finger and simulate the light absorption patterns of blood at different oxygen levels.
‚öôÔ∏è 2.1 ‚Äî Analysers & Simulators

The tester acts as a simulated finger ‚Äî it produces precise, known light absorption patterns corresponding to specific SpO‚ÇÇ percentages to check if the sensor reacts correctly.

KPIs Targeted

Precision, Accuracy & Response Time

üî¨ 2.2 ‚Äî Functional Testing

Using the simulated finger at different set oxygen levels verifies the device is functioning as intended under realistic clinical conditions ‚Äî not just in theory.

üìå Key Point: If the tester is set to 95% SpO‚ÇÇ, the device must reliably read 95% ‚Äî not 93%, not 97%.
03

Continuous Glucose Monitor (CGM)

ü©∏
Testing Equipment: Blood Glucose Strips ‚Äî these are used in two distinct ways to validate the CGM sensor. Unlike ECG or SpO‚ÇÇ testers, strips act as a reference comparison tool rather than purely a simulator.
üìê 3.1 ‚Äî Comparison with Reference Standards

The strip's reading is compared against a "gold standard" laboratory blood test ‚Äî the most trusted known value. This is the primary method to validate the CGM's output.

KPIs Targeted

Accuracy ‚Äî is the output close to the true biological value?
Precision ‚Äî do two strips from the same blood drop give the same result?

üî¨ 3.2 ‚Äî Functional Testing

Using a strip with a "control solution" ‚Äî a liquid with a precisely known glucose concentration ‚Äî confirms the sensor and strip are communicating and calculating correctly.

üìå Key Point: This is done before clinical use to rule out a faulty strip or miscalibrated sensor before it gives a patient a wrong reading.
